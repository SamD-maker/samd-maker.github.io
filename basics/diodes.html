Diodes

Diodes are semiconductor devices that allow current to flow in one direction only. They serve as protective devices in circuits by preventing reverse voltage which could damage components. Diodes are used in rectifiers, signal demodulators, and voltage regulators. Special types include light-emitting diodes (LEDs), zener diodes, and Schottky diodes. Their simplicity and reliability make them essential in power supplies and digital logic circuits.
