Capacitors

Capacitors store and release electrical energy in a circuit. They consist of two conductive plates separated by an insulating material called a dielectric. Capacitors are used in filtering, timing, energy storage, and signal coupling or decoupling. Their ability to temporarily hold a charge makes them essential in power supplies and oscillators. Common types include ceramic, electrolytic, and film capacitors, each suited for different applications based on capacitance and voltage ratings.
