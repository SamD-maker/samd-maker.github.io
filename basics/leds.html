LEDs

Light Emitting Diodes (LEDs) emit light when forward biased and current passes through them. They are energy-efficient light sources used for indication, illumination, and display applications. LEDs are available in various colors, sizes, and intensities. Unlike traditional bulbs, they consume less power, last longer, and generate less heat. They are widely used in electronic circuits as status indicators, backlights, and even in large-scale lighting installations.
