Resistors

Resistors are fundamental components in electronics that oppose the flow of electric current. They are used to control voltage and current levels, divide voltages, and protect components by limiting current. Resistors come in various values, sizes, and types including fixed, variable, and precision resistors. Color-coded bands on the body represent their resistance value and tolerance. They are found in virtually every electronic circuit and play a critical role in both analog and digital electronics.
