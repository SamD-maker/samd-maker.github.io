Transistors

Transistors are semiconductor devices used to amplify or switch electronic signals and power. They are the building blocks of modern electronic devices, enabling complex computations and signal processing. Transistors can act as digital switches or analog amplifiers. Common types include bipolar junction transistors (BJTs) and field-effect transistors (FETs). Their small size and efficiency allow them to be integrated into microchips and embedded systems. They revolutionized technology by replacing bulky vacuum tubes.
