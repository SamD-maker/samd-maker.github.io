Integrated Circuits (ICs)

Integrated Circuits (ICs) are compact assemblies of electronic components such as transistors, resistors, and capacitors built into a single semiconductor chip. They perform a variety of functions including amplification, signal processing, and computation. ICs have revolutionized electronics by enabling miniaturization, cost reduction, and improved performance. Found in everything from calculators to smartphones and computers, ICs form the brains of modern electronic systems.
